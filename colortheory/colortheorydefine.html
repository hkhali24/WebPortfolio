Color Theory Overview

Color Theory can be defined as the relationship between colors. This relationship is created in a variety of different ways such as, by mixing colors, creating color schemes etc. Click on each button to explore the 20 key color theory terms.

Tones
are created by mixing gray with a hue.
Contrast
the differences in the colors.
Vibrancy
brightness, or vividness in the color.
Saturation
the level of brightness and purity in a color.
Color scheme
all the colors that will be used in a project in a organized manner.
Monochromatic colors
follow a monotoned color theme and are colors that are adjacent to each other on the color wheel.
Neutral colors
are colors that are soft, or light colors. For example, beige and white.
Warm colors
are colors that have warmth to them. For example, red and orange have a warmer base.
Cool colors
are colors that are calming or cooling. Blue and green are a few examples of these colors.
