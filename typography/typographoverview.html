Typography Overview

Typography can be defined as applying the proper typeface at the right time. The main purpose of typography is to ensure that the message is clear and readable. Below are the 10 key typography terms and their definitions.

X-Height
is the height of a lowercase letter x and can differ among different typefaces.
Cap Height
is the height of the uppercase letter from the baseline.
Ascender Line
is the stroke of the lowercase letter that is above the x-height.
Descender Line
is the stroke of a lowercase letter that goes below the base line.
Baseline
is the line that all the letters align to.
Serif
is the corners or feet that are on the end of the letters.
Bracket
is the connecter between the serif and the stems of the letter.
Terminal
is the stoke at the end of a letter that does not have serif.
Loop
is found at the bottom of some lowercase letters, for example the letter g.
Stem
is a vertical or diagonal stroke, for example letter h has a stem.
